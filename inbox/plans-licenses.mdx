## **Overview**

The **Plans & Licensing** page allows you to view your current AI plan, monitor usage, and manage licenses for your clients.

At the top of the page, you’ll find details about your active plan, including:

- **Plan type** (AI Essentials or AI Pro)
- **Next billing date**
- **Total and used licenses** for AI and Chat
- **Ticket credits used** and refresh date
- **Unlimited AI access** is enabled or disabled

## **How It Works**

The **Manage Licenses** table lists all connected clients and their license status.

Each row includes:

- **Client name**
- **Status**
- **Company type**
- **Agreement type**
- **AI license** and **Chat license** status
- **AI credits used**

## **Setup**

Use the **actions menu (… )** on each client row—or the **Assign AI license** button—to manage access.

Available actions include:

- **Add / Remove AI license**
- **Add / Remove Chat license**

Changes take effect immediately after selection.

## **Ticket Credits**

**Ticket Credits** allow AI features to run on tickets for customers who don’t have an assigned license.

AI and Chat access are normally tied to licenses assigned to specific customers—meaning that if a customer has a license, all of their tickets qualify for AI automatically.

However, some customers—such as occasional or “break-fix” clients—may not generate enough tickets to justify a dedicated license. To support these customers, Thread provides **Ticket Credits.**

When an unlicensed customer creates a ticket that qualifies for AI, one **ticket credit** is consumed instead of blocking AI access entirely.

Partners on **AI Essentials** or **AI Pro** plans receive **three ticket credits for every license** included in their plan. Credits automatically refresh at the start of each billing cycle.

## **Best Practices**

- Regularly review license usage to ensure clients have the correct access.
- Remove unused licenses from inactive clients to keep totals accurate.
- Monitor ticket credit usage to plan for renewals or upgrades.
- If nearing your license or credit limits, consider upgrading your plan before the next billing cycle.
