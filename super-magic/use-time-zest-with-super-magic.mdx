Connect TimeZest and Super Magic can handle scheduling with your clients conversationally — look up who's bookable, check appointment types, and **send a real scheduling link to the client** without leaving the ticket.

### What you can ask

> "Send Jane a scheduling link for a 30-minute remote support session"

> "What TimeZest appointment types do we have?"

> "Who can be booked for an on-site this week?"

> "Has the client responded to the scheduling request on this ticket?"

**Heads up:** sending a scheduling request emails the client for real. That's why it always goes through Super Magic's **Confirm action** card — you'll see exactly who gets what before anything sends — and it respects your workspace's write-access settings.

#### Set it up (admin, once for the whole workspace)

TimeZest is a **workspace-level integration**: connect it once and it works for every member.

1. **Connect TimeZest to Thread** — if it isn't already, the **Configure** button on the TimeZest row in **Super Magic settings → Integrations** takes you to the setup.
2. **Enable it for Super Magic** — turn the **TimeZest** toggle on in the same Integrations card.

#### Troubleshooting

- **No scheduling tools in chat?** Check the row shows **Connected** AND the toggle is on.
- **Scheduling link won't send?** Sending is a write action — check the member passes your write-access level and the Schedule toggles are on (Tools card).
- Anything else: [**Super Magic Admin Guide → Troubleshooting**](/super-magic/super-magic-admin-guide-setup-access-safety).

#### Available tools

| Tool | Type | What it does |
| --- | --- | --- |
| list_timezest_resources | Read | Who can be booked |
| list_timezest_appointment_types | Read | Available appointment types |
| get_timezest_scheduling_requests | Read | Existing scheduling requests and their status |
| create_timezest_scheduling_request | Write | Sends a real scheduling email to the contact — always behind the Confirm action card |
